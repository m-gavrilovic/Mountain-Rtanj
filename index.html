About

Rtanj is a phenomenon that is attracting more and more tourists, scientists, adventurers and mountaineers year after year. Everyone who has visited this mountain at least once claims that they have felt inexplicable energy, and that the nature that surrounds it seems completely unreal. Some claim to have healed themselves with the help of famous Rtanj tea, while others have found peace here. The adventure that accompanies the walk and tour of Rtanj is invaluable. The alternation of dense forests and rocky landscapes, flat and bumpy surfaces, and abundance of medicinal plants is what you can experience if you decide to meet this mountain. The view that stretches for miles around and the clean air awaits you at the top as a reward.

As a symbol of the mountain, at the top of Šiljak (1565m) there is another curiosity that tourists are happy to visit. These are the ruins of a former church - the chapel of St. George. In addition to being located on the highest peak of Rtanj, this church is interesting because in 1935, with the help of 1000 workers in honor of her husband, it was built by the wife of the owner of the Rtanj mine after his death. Today, it is in ruins, as gold diggers have repeatedly destroyed it with the help of dynamite. In addition to the former church - the chapel, another of the must-see tourist attractions of Rtanj is the ice cave, located on the southeast side of the highest peak of this mountain. Covered with clay and stone blocks, fifty meters long, it is also the only freezer in the karst of Eastern Serbia where low air temperatures are recorded.
